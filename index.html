Events

1st March

Aiburobhat 1PM - 3PM

Aiburobhat is a Bengali wedding tradition that involves a lavish feast for the bride and groom at their parents' home. The name loosely translates to "Aiburo (bachelor) Bhat (rice) Aiburobhat can be similar to a bachelor party. It is considered to be very important for the bride.

Mehndi & Sangeet 6PM - 9PM

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together and a professional mehndi artist applies mehndi to the bride's hands and feet. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more. With booze flowing down the floor, everyone would be busy showing off their new moves on the stage which they have finely honed.

2nd March

Haldi 10AM-11AM

It is celebrated on the wedding day or one day prior to the wedding day and is usually a close-knitted event. The closest family, relatives and friends come together on this auspicious occasion to apply turmeric paste (haldi) on the bride and groom's face and body. The occasion is celebrated with dhol (drum beats), Bollywood songs and endless dancing.

Wedding 6PM-12AM

Moving on to the actual wedding celebration, the function begins with the mounting of the Baraat. The groom along with his family and friends move towards the wedding venue. It is like a mini festival in itself with everyone dancing to the beat of the dhol or music. With hugs, garlands and tikas, the groom's side is welcomed into the bride's home or the venue. Once the bride and groom are at the altar, they finally exchange garlands and tie the knot. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.
